Understanding Relationship Patterns

Most relationship struggles aren't about the other person — they're about the subconscious patterns you bring to every relationship. These patterns typically form in childhood: how love was modelled, how attachment was experienced, what you learned about trust, worthiness, and safety. Your subconscious created a "relationship template" based on early experiences — and you've been unconsciously selecting partners, reacting, and relating according to that template ever since.

This is why self-help books and conscious effort often fail — the template operates below awareness. You can intellectually understand what you "should" do while your subconscious automatically recreates the familiar pattern. This is the root of attachment styles (anxious, avoidant, disorganized), inner child wounds affecting adult relationships, and sometimes even past-life relationship patterns that continue to repeat. Many couples also carry inherited family patterns — patterns passed down through generations without anyone knowing why.

Breakup and divorce healing works similarly. The pain often connects to deeper wounds — rejection, abandonment, self-worth — that predate the current relationship. The relationship was simply the trigger that activated these older, unhealed wounds. When you address those roots, the grief transforms.
